Description

CSC110 is an introduction to programming using the Java programming language. The primary focus of this class is developing problem-solving skills and a general knowledge of programming that will be useful to you in most programming languages. While we will spend a lot of time learning about Java and how to write good code, writing code isn't our ultimate goal. The most important skill you can learn in this class is how to think like a programmer. Once you've mastered that, writing code is surprisingly easy.

Objectives

CSC110 and CSC110L share objectives. Upon successful completion of both courses, students should be able to:

  1. Discuss the challenges inherent in communicating with (programming) a (non-reasoning) computer.

  2. Break a complex problem down into smaller more manageable components.

  3. Provide step-by-step instructions to solve small computational problems.

  4. Using at least one programming language, write examples of and solve problems in programs with the following basic programming constructs and tools:

    1. Mathematical expressions
    2. Conditional expressions
    3. Simple iterative statements
    4. Simple functions
    5. Simple data structures, such as arrays, lists, and strings

  5. Discuss the importance of commenting and good code structure/style

  • Individual assignments are marked either complete or incomplete depending on whether they meet a set of requirements.
  • Assignments can be revised and resubmitted multiple times up until their deadline. An incomplete means "try again," not "you failed."

Deadlines

Submission Limit and Assignment Deadlines

You have a limited number of attempts for autograded assignments, and each attempt has a deadline. You may submit your work any number of times before that deadline, but I when I grade the attempt I will only use your most recent on-time submission.

You are allowed one attempt at each autograded assignment per week, due by 11:59 PM ET on Sunday. I may allow extra attempts later in the semester depending on how the first couple of projects go. If I do, then I will announce the deadlines for these attempts on Blackboard. This means you can retry an assignment at least once per week until its deadline.

Assignment deadlines will be posted on Blackboard when the assignment is announced. A deadline is your last chance to submit the assignment. You will not be allowed to retry after the deadline.

Both the attempt deadlines and assignment deadlines will be strictly enforced. This keeps them simple and consistent, and it should not be a problem unless you wait until the last minute to turn something in.

Blackboard Submissions

All work must be submitted on Blackboard. You must attach your source code in a .java file named exactly as specified in the assignment.

Feedback on Autograded Assignments

Most assignments are autograded. I will run your program through a series of tests, and if it passes every test then it will be marked complete. If it fails one or more tests, then it will be marked incomplete. My tests will also produce feedback if they fail, and I will share this feedback with you when I update assignment grades. This feedback should help you revise your program so that it passes the test next time.

The tests have very specific requirements, and you should expect to fail all or most of them on your first attempt. Plan on resubmitting your work at least two or three times before your program passes every test. This means submitting your first attempt several weeks before the deadline.

Extensions

In rare cases (such as medical or family emergencies), I will allow an extension to a deadline or an exception to another restriction on submitting an assignment. You can email me to ask for a such an exception if you believe one is appropriate in your situation. Your email should include an explanation of why you need the extension, a new proposed deadline (or the proposed exception to a policy), and your current progress on the assignment. Be sure to send me your code and any other material you've created for the assignment so far.

I have some expectations if I am going to give an extension or make an exception to a class policy:

  • You've demonstrated that you read this policy by including all of the information I just asked for.
  • If you're having trouble with the assignment, then you've taken steps to get help. Usually this means seeing a tutor or going to office hours.
  • You are clearly making an effort to keep up with the class and not procrastinating. If there is an ongoing problem affecting your work or participation in the class and you want me to accommodate you, then you must let me know early and keep me informed as that situation develops.

Extensions and other exceptions are given on a case-by-case basis. I will try to be as fair as possible, but this comes down to my judgement and whether you've been proactively communicating with me.

FAQ

  • If I'm not graded on an assignment, does that mean I don't need to put any effort into it? I still review assignments and check that tasks were completed as directed. You should expect to be challenged and have to put in a significant amount of work.
  • Why can't I get an A+? QU's grading system does not include an A+. The highest grade you can earn in any QU class is an A.
  • Why can't I get an A-, B+, B-, C+, or C-? There aren't enough assignments to include an option for every in-between grade, and they're not necessary. An A is the highest grade you can earn, a B is less demanding but still a good grade, and a C represents the minimum requirements to pass this class and meet the prerequisite for other classes.
  • Is there extra credit? No.
  • Can I get partial credit for an assignment? No. You must complete the entire assignment, submit it on time, and submit it in the correct format to receive credit for it. If you're having trouble, then please ask me or a tutor for help.
  • Are there exams? No. Graded exams, quizzes, and similar assessments don't work with this grading system. There are no exams, not even a final exam. However, I may give ungraded quizzes as a way for you to check your understanding of topics we've covered.

If you have questions about the grading system that aren't answered here, then please don't hesitate to ask me about it! I want you to feel comfortable with the grading system and understand what's required to earn the grade you want.

Labor-Based Grading

What is Labor-Based Grading?

Labor-based grading and contract grading are approaches to grading that try to emphasize the work you put into a class over the outcomes of that work. This grading system specifies what work you need to complete to earn a particular final grade, and these individual units of work aren't usually graded. You receive a final grade based on how much work you complete rather than a weighted average of grades from assignments and exams.

There are plenty of articles online about labor-based grading if you want to read more about it. Here's one that was easy to find and that I think explains the concept well: https://cep.barnard.edu/labor-based-grading. This article also links to an example grade contract. My implementation will differ, but my goals are largely the same.

Why Labor-Based Grading?

The author of the example grade contract shown in the Bernard College webpage gives an excellent explanation of why they prefer contract grading over traditional grades. I recommend reading that if you want to hear more about the motivation behind instructors using labor-based grading. It doesn't fix all of the problems with grades, but I think it at least mitigates some of them.

Unauthorized Possession of Materials

If you have not completed an assignment, then you must not at any point access or possess a full or partial solution to that assignment. This includes material shared by a classmate, posted online, or generated by an AI.

AI Use and Plagiarism

The following are not allowed and will be reported as a violation of the AIA policy:

  • Copying, paraphrasing, or otherwise basing any significant part of your work off code you did not write. This includes AI-generated code, code found online, and code written by a classmate.
  • Allowing someone else to do your work for you, dictate code to you, or otherwise do most of the actual work. If your only contribution is typing, then it's not your work.
  • Splitting work between multiple people or otherwise working together on an assignment that doesn't explicitly allow group work.

The following are allowed:

  • Using material I provide you. If I include code in an assignment, show you code during class/office hours, or share code in my notes or other material, then you're free to use it in any assignments for this class.
  • Asking other people for help, asking an AI for help, and looking up online sources for help. However, your use of AI/other sources and the help you receive from other people must not violate any other rules in this policy. Any assistance should focus on helping you understand the problem you're trying to solve and the programming concepts you need to use, not on writing your code.

If the help you receive from someone, your use of AI, or your use of other sources results in work that looks like it was plagiarized, AI-generated, or otherwise in violation of the AIA policy, then it will be treated as an AIA violation.

Sharing Work

You're free to share or publicly post work completed for this class for any of the following reasons:

  • To include in a portfolio
  • To seek help or feedback from someone outside of the class
  • To seek help or feedback from a classmate who has already completed that assignment
  • To discuss an assignment with someone outside of the class or a classmate who has already completed that assignment

I'm open to sharing for other reasons, but you should check with me first to make sure this won't later be viewed as an AIA violation.

I also want to specify reasons for sharing that will always be considered an AIA violation:

  • Posting or sharing work for the purpose of facilitating an AIA violation
  • Sharing work with someone you know has not completed that assignment

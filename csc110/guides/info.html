Computer Information

You should have a Linux, macOS, or Windows laptop (Chromebooks are not suitable for this class). Aside from the operating system (Linux, Mac, or Windows), there are a few other things you'll need to know about your computer.

Architecture

When downloading software, you may need to know what kind of CPU your computer has (the CPU's "architecture") to choose the correct version of that software. You most likely have an x86_64 (abbreviated x64) CPU or an aarch64 (also called arm64) CPU. To check:

  • Linux: Open your terminal and run uname -m.
  • Mac: Check About This Mac. Older Macs have x86_64 CPU's, and newer Mac's have aarch64 CPUs.
  • Windows: Open "About your PC" in system settings and check "System type". You most likely have an x86_64 CPU if you're using Windows.

Home Directory

Your home directory is where your terminal should start and where you'll find many familiar folders like Documents and Desktop. You can run echo $HOME on your bash or zsh terminal to print the path of your home directory. Depending on your operating system, this will normally be:

  • Linux: /home/username
  • Mac: /Users/username
  • Windows: /c/Users/username
    • Windows formats paths differently from Linux and Mac, so you may also see this displayed as C:\Users\username outside of your Git Bash terminal.

Note: "username" is a placeholder for your actual username. My username is "zach" and I have a Linux computer, so my home directory is /home/zach.

CSC110 Assignments

Final Project

The final project is an open-ended assignment. You're free to explore any topic that interests you, as long as it is relevant to CSC110 and you can produce a working program to demo at the end of the semester.

This assignment counts as two normal assignments for the purpose of final grades:

  • Submitting the program and other required material on time counts as one completed assignment
  • Participating in the in-class demos and discussing your program with me counts as one completed assignment

Groups

You can complete this project on your own or work in a group. Groups must be established in your proposal, and all group members must be present when making the proposal.

Proposal

Before you begin the project, you must present the idea for your project during class or during my in-person office hours. I will reserve some time at the end of class meetings for these proposals.

A proposal should take no more than five minutes to present and must answer the questions below. You can format this information however you want as long as it can be displayed with your laptop.

  1. Who is working on the project?
  2. What will your program do when the project is complete?
  3. What do you expect to learn from creating this program? Different group members may have different learning goals depending on how the work is divided.
  4. If working in a group, what is the primary responsibility of each group member? Everyone must contribute substantially to the code written for a group project. I expect group projects to be large enough that each member can "own" a specific part of the program, even if other portions are a shared responsibility.
  5. What are some concrete objectives that can be used to assess whether you have completed the project? These are the criteria I will use to grade the submitted program.

I may ask for changes before approving your project. When assessing a proposal, I will consider the following:

  • Did you address everything listed above?
  • Are your objectives clear enough that I can use them to assess your finished program?
  • Does the project have a clear and reasonable scope? Projects that are too ambitious to complete in a few weeks or so small as to be trivial aren't suitable for this assignment. Let's assume you will work on this for three weeks and spend 2-5 hours per week. That means it should involve somewhere from 5 to 15 hours of work per group member to complete. This does not include unproductive time, like spending two hours debugging a small problem that would have taken a few minutes if you had asked for help. If you spend time poorly, then expect to spend significantly more than the estimated 5-15 hours.

Final projects will not be accepted without an approved proposal. If, while working on the project, you wish to make significant changes from what was covered in your proposal, then you must present an updated proposal for the new version by the proposal deadline. If you don't do this, or if I don't approve the new version, then you will be held to the version that I did approve.

All material used for your presentation must be uploaded to Blackboard before you present it. If we don't have enough time for everyone who wishes to present on a given day, then I will prioritize groups based on how early their material was uploaded to Blackboard.

Finally, a reminder not to use an LLM or other AI tool to write your proposal or generate material for a proposal. I want to hear you explain your idea for your project. Unauthorized AI use or plagiarism on the proposal will be treated just as seriously as if you had cheated on the program.

Demo (1 Point)

At the end of the semester, either during the last week of class or during our final exam period, we will demo final projects. This will be an informal process: each group who completed a project should have their program ready to run on a laptop, and all students will be free to wander around and check out other project demos. Be prepared to explain your program, show how it works, and, if you're comfortable doing so, allow other people to try using it.

I will look at every project during this time and ask each group to demonstrate their finished program. Presenting your work on this day is worth 1 completed assignment. To receive credit, you must:

  1. Be present for this class meeting.
  2. Have a working program that matches what you described in your proposal.
  3. Help demonstrate your program to me and answer questions about it.

If you cannot attend the demo and want to schedule a make-up, then I expect you to notify me as early as possible and explain why you cannot attend. I will only allow make-ups if notified promptly and if the absence was necessary (not, for example, because you decided to go on a vacation that day or wanted to leave early for winter break).

Program Submission (1 Point)

Your finished program must be submitted on Blackboard before the deadline. This submission on its own is worth 1 completed assignment, provided the following requirements are met:

  1. The program is submitted on time.
  2. The program compiles, runs, and is what you described in your proposal. It must meet your stated objectives or be reasonably close to meeting each objective.
  3. Your program should be runnable with the java command on your main source file. If it requires additional steps to compile/run, then include compile/run instructions with your submission.
  4. Your program should provide clear prompts and directions for the user. If a user would need to know something that the program doesn't explain to them, then include instructions for using the program with your submission. The user should not have to read your code to understand how to use the program.
  5. If your program has significant bugs or there was a problem meeting some of your objectives, then you must include an explanation of these issues with the submission. I expect to see a good faith effort towards completing each objective, and the program must be close to meeting each objective even if you ran into issues.
  6. If you worked in a group, then your submission must include a summary of each member's contributions to the project. These must be consistent with the individual responsibilities given in your proposal.
